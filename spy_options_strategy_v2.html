🔄 The Two Strategies

Cash-Secured Put (CSP)

Sell a put, hold full cash to buy 100 SPY shares if assigned. More premium, more capital tied up, downside to $0.

Bull Put Spread (BPS)

Sell a put + buy a lower put. Less premium but defined max loss. Less capital required. Better in uncertain markets.

SPY stays above strike → keep full premium ✅ SPY falls below (CSP) → assigned, own SPY shares → start Wheel SPY falls below (BPS) → max loss = spread width - premium collected

📅 The Master Decision Flow

Open position (30–45 DTE) ↓ Set GTC limit buy at 50% of premium immediately ↓ Monitor daily inside 21 DTE ↓ ├── 50% profit hit (any time)? → Close immediately, redeploy │ ├── 21 DTE reached, in profit? → Close anyway (gamma risk) │ ├── 21 DTE reached, at a LOSS? → Must still act — do NOT wait │ │ │ ├── Loss < 1× premium → Roll down and out (lower strike, further expiry) │ ├── Loss = 2× premium → Close immediately — stop loss triggered │ └── Loss > 2× premium → Close or accept assignment │ ├── Strike approaching (any DTE)? → Roll / accept assignment / cut loss │ └── Put worth <$0.10? → Let expire (commission > remaining risk) ⚠️ Waiting past 21 DTE for a bounce is NOT an option. Inside 21 DTE, gamma dominates — a 1% SPY drop can move your put 4–5× more than it would outside 21 DTE.

🏗️ How the Bull Put Spread Is Constructed

Two legs. The short put earns income. The long put caps your downside.

SELL higher put ← income leg (chosen via delta, same as CSP) BUY lower put ← protection leg (what this tab covers) Spread width = short strike - long strike Max profit = net premium collected Max loss = (spread width × 100) - net premium Capital req. = spread width × 100

📊 Factor 2 — Return on Capital (ROC)

Don't just look at raw premium — measure what you earn relative to what you risk.

ROC = Net Premium ÷ Max Loss $600/$595 spread: $100 ÷ $400 = 25% ROC $600/$590 spread: $180 ÷ $820 = 22% ROC $600/$585 spread: $300 ÷ $1,200 = 25% ROC ← sweet spot $600/$575 spread: $450 ÷ $2,050 = 22% ROC Target: ROC of 20–25% per cycle at 30–45 DTE

📍 Factor 3 — Where to Place the Long Put

Approach A — Fixed Width (Simpler)

Pick a standard spread width ($10–$15) and stick to it every trade. Keeps position sizing consistent and easy to manage. Best for beginners.

Approach B — Key Support Level

Look at SPY's chart and place the long put just below a major support level. Makes the spread structurally meaningful — protection kicks in exactly where the market breaks down.

Example (Approach B): SPY at $655, short put at $600 Chart shows strong SPY support at $580–585 → Buy $580 put as protection Logic: If SPY breaks $580, something has gone seriously wrong — that's exactly when you want your protection leg to kick in.

⚠️ Why Not Wait for 80–90% in a Flat Market?
The last 10–20% of premium only decays in the final 5–7 days — right when gamma risk is highest. A single 2% SPY drop in that window can erase weeks of patient waiting.

The rule applies regardless of market condition. Market conditions only determine HOW QUICKLY you reach 50%, not whether you use the rule.

"50% profit OR 21 DTE remaining — whichever comes first, close it. Market condition only determines how fast you get there."

🚫 Why Not Let Options Expire Worthless?

Sold put for $6.00. Looks almost free in final week... Day 23: put worth $0.80 ← "almost there!" Day 24: SPY drops 2% → put jumps to $2.50 Day 25: SPY drops 1% → put jumps to $5.00 Day 30: You scramble to close at $4.50 Final profit: $1.50 instead of $5.20 ❌ This is the gamma trap.

Only Let Expire If:

1
You intentionally WANT assignment (running the Wheel)
2
Put is worth $0.05–0.10 — commission to close costs more than risk remaining

🔄 When and How to Roll

Rolling = close current position + open new one. Always aim for a net credit.

ScenarioRoll Action
SPY drops, still 2+ weeks to expiryRoll out in time — same strike, later expiry
SPY drops sharply, near expiryRoll down AND out — lower strike, later expiry
SPY rallies, 50% profit hitClose early, open fresh position
VIX spikes mid-tradeConsider closing — IV crush will help after the spike

⚙️ GTC Order — Set It and Forget It

You sell put for $6.00 → Immediately place GTC limit buy order at $3.00 When put decays to $3.00, it closes automatically. No need to watch it daily. Capital freed → redeploy immediately.

⏰ 21 DTE Reached — What If I'm at a Loss?

21 DTE is a hard action trigger — not a suggestion. Waiting for a bounce is not an option because gamma accelerates losses rapidly inside this window.

⚠️ Important Rule on Rolling at a Loss
Never roll just to avoid realising a loss. Only roll if:
1. You genuinely believe SPY will stabilise or recover
2. You're comfortable being assigned at the new lower strike

If the market is in a clear downtrend (sustained selloff), closing for a defined loss and waiting for a better re-entry is smarter than rolling deeper into trouble.

⚡ Gamma Risk — The Final Week Trap

Inside 21 DTE, your position changes character: BEFORE 21 DTE → Theta-driven (slow, predictable decay) ✅ AFTER 21 DTE → Gamma-driven (explosive sensitivity to moves) ⚠️ A delta 0.20 strike can become delta 0.50 overnight if SPY drops 3% in the final week. This is why 21 DTE is the hard close rule — no exceptions.
