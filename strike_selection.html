⚠️ Universal Rules — Apply to Every Strategy Below

1
CC and BPS: Always enter at 45 DTE. You need the full runway before the 21 DTE hard-close trigger. Never sell below 30 DTE.
2
Sell Put DTE depends on your goal. Income-focused → 45 DTE (50% GTC + 21 DTE close apply). Assignment-focused → 21–30 DTE (no GTC, no 21 DTE close — hold to expiry). See each Sell Put tab for full detail.
3

4

5
Stop loss at 2× premium collected. Hard rule for CC and BPS. Soft reassessment trigger for assignment-focused puts — ask: do I still want these shares at this price in this market?
6
VIX > 35 → pause ALL new positions. No SPY, no QQQ, no CC, no puts, no BPS. Market dislocation — wait for calm.
7
No new positions the week before Fed, CPI, or major earnings. QQQ strategies: also avoid big-tech earnings weeks (AAPL, MSFT, NVDA, META, AMZN, GOOGL).

SPY Covered Call

🔴 The Core Tension

Covered calls on SPY have one fundamental conflict: you want to hold SPY long-term, but a call caps your upside and can force you to sell. The two-bucket system resolves this.

Core Bucket

🏔️ Long-Term Hold

Shares you intend to hold for years. Sell calls here very conservatively — or not at all. Upside preservation is the priority.

Wheel Bucket

🔄 Active Income

Shares you are OK selling if called away. Assignment here is a success — cash feeds back into the sell put / BPS cycle.

🎯 Core Bucket — Strike Criteria

Goal: Minimal income, maximum upside preservation If ANY doubt about losing these shares → go higher or skip Delta: 0.10–0.15 OTM%: 12–15% above current SPY price DTE: 45 days Strike floor: Must feel comfortable even if SPY runs 20%+ above it VIX adjustment: VIX <18 → 12% OTM minimum VIX 18–25 → 15% OTM minimum VIX >25 → Skip — vol is elevated, don't risk assignment GTC: 50% profit — set immediately 21 DTE: Hard close Assignment: Do NOT auto-resell CC if called away Re-evaluate — rebuild core position first

Example (SPY $555, VIX ~20)

12% OTM → $555 × 1.12 = $621 strike minimum Delta check: should be ~0.12–0.14 Premium: ~$0.80–1.20/contract (intentionally small) GTC: set at $0.40–0.60 (50%) One-line test: "Would I regret losing these shares at $621?" If ANY doubt → go to $640+ or skip entirely

🎯 Wheel Bucket — Strike Criteria

Goal: Maximise premium, assignment is welcome and expected Assignment feeds cash back into sell put / BPS cycle Delta: 0.20–0.25 OTM%: 8–12% above current SPY price DTE: 45 days Strike floor: Must be ABOVE your cost basis on these shares VIX adjustment: VIX <18 → 8% OTM VIX 18–25 → 10% OTM VIX 25–35 → 12% OTM (widen, don't tighten) VIX >35 → Pause all new CC positions GTC: 50% profit — set immediately 21 DTE: Hard close Stop loss: Buy back if loss = 2× premium collected Assignment: Success → cash returns to sell put / BPS cycle

Example (SPY $555, VIX ~20)

10% OTM → $555 × 1.10 = $610 strike Delta check: should be ~0.20–0.22 Premium: ~$2.50–3.50/contract GTC: set at $1.25–1.75 (50%) One-line test: "Am I genuinely happy selling these shares at $610?" If yes → sell. Assignment here = Wheel working.

"Wheel bucket assignment is not a failure — it's the cycle completing. Cash out, sell a put, get assigned back in lower, repeat."

SPY Sell Put

🔄 Two Modes — Choose Before You Trade

Your intent determines the entire setup. Decide which mode you're in before placing the order — they use different DTE, different management rules, and different GTC behaviour.

Mode 1 — Income

💰 Collect Premium

Goal is to close at 50% profit and redeploy. Assignment is incidental — goes to Wheel bucket if it happens.

Mode 2 — Assignment

🎯 Buy the Dip

Goal is to be assigned at your target price. Hold to expiry intentionally. Goes straight to Core bucket on assignment.

💰 Mode 1 — Income-Focused Put

Goal: Collect premium efficiently, close early, redeploy Assignment is coincidental — not the plan Delta: 0.15–0.20 OTM%: See VIX table below DTE: 45 days (need runway before 21 DTE trigger) Strike floor: A price you'd be OK owning SPY at if assigned VIX adjustment: VIX <18 → 5–7% below SPY VIX 18–25 → 8–10% below SPY VIX 25–35 → 10–12% below SPY VIX >35 → Pause GTC: 50% profit — set immediately on entry ✅ 21 DTE: Hard close or roll — no exceptions ✅ Stop loss: Close if loss = 2× premium collected Assignment: Incidental → Wheel bucket

Example (SPY $555, VIX ~22, Mode 1)

9% OTM → $555 × 0.91 = $505 strike, 45 DTE Premium: ~$4.00–5.50/contract GTC: set at $2.00–2.75 (50%) immediately If assigned: 100 SPY shares → Wheel bucket → sell CC

🎯 Mode 2 — Assignment-Focused Put

Goal: Get assigned at your target dip price for Core bucket You WANT to own these 100 SPY shares long-term Delta: 0.20–0.30 (higher delta = more likely to be assigned) OTM%: Place at your specific target buy price Not a % rule — pick the price YOU want to own SPY at DTE: 21–30 days (shorter — faster assignment, faster reassessment) Strike floor: Must be a price you'd be happy holding for years GTC: NOT needed — you want to hold to expiry ❌ 21 DTE: NOT a close trigger — you're watching for assignment ❌ Stop loss: Soft reassessment only — if SPY is in freefall, ask: "Do I still want SPY at this price right now?" Close and re-enter at a lower strike if market is breaking down Assignment: The whole point → Core bucket → hold long-term If expires worthless: Re-sell immediately at same strike next cycle

Example (SPY $555, VIX ~22, Mode 2)

You want to buy SPY at $500 (a genuine dip target) → Sell SPY $500 put, 21–30 DTE → Collect ~$2.50–3.50 premium → No GTC order needed — just monitor SPY falls to $495 → assigned 100 shares at $500 ✅ Effective cost: $500 – $3.00 = $497 → Core bucket SPY stays above $500 → expires worthless Keep $300 premium → sell again next 21–30 DTE cycle Repeat until assigned at your target price
⚠ Why Shorter DTE for Assignment Mode?
If SPY is going to dip to your level, it typically happens within 3–4 weeks — not 6 weeks. Shorter cycles let you reassess your target price more frequently. If SPY rallies away from your strike, you can adjust without being locked into a 45-day commitment. Premium per cycle is lower, but similar annualised since you cycle twice as often.

SPY Bull Put Spread

🎯 What Makes BPS Different from Sell Put

Sell Put

Full Capital Required

Broker holds ~$55,500 as collateral per contract (full share price). High capital requirement.

BPS

Spread Width Only

Broker holds only the spread width as collateral. $15-wide BPS = $1,500 max collateral. Run alongside share holdings.

BPS is your tool for when all capital is deployed in shares but you still want premium income. The defined max loss also removes the assignment risk entirely.

🎯 Short Put (Income Leg) — Strike Criteria

This leg is identical to the Sell Put criteria above. The short put is your income source. Delta (short put): 0.15–0.20 OTM%: VIX <18 → 5–7% below SPY VIX 18–25 → 8–10% below SPY VIX 25–35 → 10–12% below SPY VIX >35 → Pause DTE: 45 days Strike test: "Would I want to own SPY at this price?" (Even though BPS can't assign, this keeps your strike discipline honest)

🎯 Long Put (Protection Leg) — Strike Criteria

The long put caps your downside. Two approaches: Approach A — Fixed Width (Simpler, recommended): Use $10–$15 spread width consistently Short put at $505 → long put at $490–$495 Keeps position sizing consistent across trades Approach B — Key Support Level: Look at SPY chart → find major support below short put Place long put just below that support level Logic: protection kicks in where market truly breaks down Target ROC (Return on Capital): Net premium ÷ (spread width × 100) = 20–25% If ROC < 15% → spread too wide, find tighter width If ROC > 35% → short put too close, risk too high

Example (SPY $555, VIX ~22)

Short put: $505 (9% OTM, delta ~0.18) Long put: $490 ($15-wide spread) Capital: $1,500 per spread Premium: ~$3.00–4.00 net credit Max loss: ($15 × 100) – $350 = $1,150 ROC: $350 ÷ $1,500 = 23% ✅ GTC buy-back: $1.50–2.00 (50% of premium) Stop loss: Close if spread costs 2× premium to exit Sold for $3.50 → close if costs $10.50

🚨 BPS Stop Loss — "Never Let It Fulfill"

Three layers of protection — all three active simultaneously: Layer 1 — GTC 50% profit (auto-close on profit): Set immediately on entry. Fires when premium halves. Layer 2 — 21 DTE hard close (calendar-based): Set a reminder the day you open every trade. Close at 21 DTE regardless of P&L. Layer 3 — Stop loss at 2× premium (loss limit): Collected $3.50 → close if spread costs $10.50 to exit. Check manually if SPY drops sharply mid-trade. The only gap risk: SPY gaps 5%+ overnight → stop loss may not fill cleanly Solution: VIX >35 → pause, no new BPS positions SPY almost never gaps >5% overnight in normal conditions
⚠ Position Sizing Rule
Never use more than 15–20% of total portfolio as BPS collateral. On a $100,000 portfolio → max $15,000–20,000 tied up in BPS. A worst-case month where all spreads hit stop loss should not materially damage your long-term compounding.

QQQ Sell Put

🔄 Two Modes — Choose Before You Trade

Same two-mode framework as SPY but with one extra layer of discipline: QQQ's tech concentration means macro events and earnings can gap it 4–5% overnight. Check the calendar before every trade regardless of mode.

Mode 1 — Income

💰 Collect Premium

45 DTE, GTC at 50%, 21 DTE close. Assignment incidental → Wheel bucket. More premium than SPY for same delta.

Mode 2 — Assignment

🎯 Buy the Dip

21–30 DTE, no GTC, hold to expiry. Assignment planned → Core bucket. Re-sell same strike if expires worthless.

💰 Mode 1 — Income-Focused Put

Goal: Collect premium efficiently, close at 50%, redeploy Delta: 0.15–0.20 OTM%: See VIX table below (slightly wider than SPY) DTE: 45 days Strike floor: A price you'd be OK owning 100 QQQ at if assigned VIX adjustment: VIX <18 → 7–9% below QQQ VIX 18–25 → 10–12% below QQQ VIX 25–35 → 12–15% below QQQ VIX >35 → Pause Macro blackout: No new puts the week before: Fed · CPI · AAPL, MSFT, NVDA, META, AMZN, GOOGL earnings GTC: 50% profit — set immediately on entry ✅ 21 DTE: Hard close or roll — no exceptions ✅ Stop loss: Close if loss = 2× premium collected Assignment: Incidental → Wheel bucket

Example (QQQ $470, VIX ~22, Mode 1)

11% OTM → $470 × 0.89 = $418 strike, 45 DTE Premium: ~$5.00–7.00/contract GTC: set at $2.50–3.50 (50%) immediately If assigned: 100 QQQ shares → Wheel bucket → sell CC

🎯 Mode 2 — Assignment-Focused Put

Goal: Get assigned at your Nasdaq dip target for Core bucket Delta: 0.20–0.30 OTM%: Place at your specific target buy price for QQQ DTE: 21–30 days (shorter for faster assignment + reassessment) Strike floor: A price you'd be happy holding QQQ at for years Macro blackout: CRITICAL for Mode 2 — a tech earnings gap can push QQQ through your strike violently, not cleanly. Never open Mode 2 puts in the week before: Fed · CPI · AAPL, MSFT, NVDA, META, AMZN, GOOGL earnings GTC: NOT needed — hold to expiry ❌ 21 DTE: NOT a close trigger ❌ Stop loss: Soft reassessment — if market is breaking down structurally, close and re-enter at lower strike Assignment: The whole point → Core bucket → hold long-term If expires worthless: Re-sell same strike next 21–30 DTE cycle

Example (QQQ $470, VIX ~22, Mode 2)

You want to own QQQ at $420 (genuine dip target) → Sell QQQ $420 put, 21–30 DTE → Collect ~$3.00–4.00 premium → No GTC — hold and watch QQQ falls to $415 → assigned 100 shares at $420 ✅ Effective cost: $420 – $3.50 = $416.50 → Core bucket QQQ stays above $420 → expires worthless Keep $350 premium → sell again immediately at same strike
⚠ Extra QQQ Caution in Mode 2
QQQ can gap 4–5% in a single session on a big-tech earnings miss. In Mode 2, you're holding through expiry — a gap can put you deep ITM before you can act. If you open a Mode 2 put and a major tech earnings event is announced before expiry, consider closing early and re-entering after the event clears.

🚨 QQQ BPS — Extra Gap Risk Warning

QQQ BPS carries more overnight gap risk than SPY BPS. Why: QQQ = Nasdaq-100. Top 6 stocks = ~50% of index. If NVDA misses earnings → QQQ can gap 4–5% overnight Your spread goes from comfortable OTM → deep ITM instantly Stop loss cannot protect against overnight gaps Mitigation rules: 1. Never open QQQ BPS within 1 week of big tech earnings 2. Use $10–$15 wide spreads max — limits max loss 3. Follow 15–20% portfolio cap on total BPS collateral 4. 🚨 If QQQ gaps against your BPS overnight
Do NOT wait to see if it recovers. Close at market open. A deep ITM spread on QQQ can hit full max loss within days inside 21 DTE. Take the defined loss and move on — that's what the spread width is for.
